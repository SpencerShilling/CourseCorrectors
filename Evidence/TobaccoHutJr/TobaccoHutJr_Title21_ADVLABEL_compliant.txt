Tobacco Hut Junior Advertising and Labeling Compliance Review Date: July 15, 2025 Prepared by: Retail Compliance and Audit Team
Purpose This document provides evidence confirming Tobacco Hut Junior’s compliance with the advertising and labeling requirements established under 21 CFR §1140.32(b). The audit examined print, in-store, and digital marketing materials for the presence, format, and placement of Surgeon General’s warnings.
Evidence Summary Audit Scope — A total of 120 marketing and product display materials were reviewed across seven store locations and corporate digital channels.
Design Review — Each advertisement and display included the required Surgeon General’s warning statement covering at least 20 percent of the advertisement area, consistent with contrast and border specifications.
Labeling Accuracy — No discrepancies were found in warning language, font size, or positioning relative to the advertising text.
Quality Assurance Controls — Marketing materials undergo internal compliance review prior to distribution. Documentation confirmed that the QA process met the requirements of §1140.32(b)(1)–(5).
Retention and Archiving — All reviewed proofs, approvals, and QA sign-offs were archived per §1140.32(c) in the Marketing Compliance Repository.
Supporting Documentation
	1	Advertising Proofs — Image files and layout samples showing compliant warning placement.
	2	QA Review Logs — Sign-off records for each campaign and store display.
	3	Marketing SOP — Version 3.2 outlining compliance with FDA advertising standards.
	4	Archival Records — Confirmation of retention and accessibility of approved materials.
Observations
	•	All reviewed advertisements and packaging displayed warnings in accordance with FDA specifications.
	•	Internal QA review process ensures sustained compliance.
	•	No corrective actions or follow-up audits required.
