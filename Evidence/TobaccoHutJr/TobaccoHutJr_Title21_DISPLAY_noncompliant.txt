obacco Hut Junior Self-Service Display Violations Date: February 10, 2025 Prepared by: Retail Compliance and Audit Team
Purpose This document provides evidence of noncompliance with 21 CFR §1140.16(c), which prohibits self-service displays of tobacco products. The audit identified stores allowing customer access to tobacco products without employee supervision.
Evidence Summary Inspection Findings — Three store locations were observed with open cigarette display racks accessible to customers.
Policy Documentation — Store policies incorrectly defined “restricted access” areas, allowing partial self-service for tobacco accessories.
Visual Confirmation — Audit photos confirmed customers were able to handle tobacco products without employee involvement.
Regulatory Reference — §1140.16(c) explicitly prohibits the display or self-service availability of tobacco products.
Corrective Measures — Removal of open display racks and staff retraining were mandated immediately following inspection.
Supporting Documentation
	1	Store Inspection Reports — Detailing store layouts and display conditions.
	2	Policy Manual Excerpts — Outdated provisions allowing customer access.
	3	Photographic Evidence — Audit images verifying self-service violations.
	4	Corrective Action Records — Store-level confirmations of display removal.
Observations
	•	Customer-accessible displays violate §1140.16(c).
	•	Policy definitions remain inconsistent with federal regulations.
	•	Immediate corrective measures implemented to restore compliance.
