Settlement Smashers
Authority to Provide Consulting Services
Date: April 9, 2025
Prepared by: Professional Services Compliance and Operations Team

Purpose
This document provides evidence confirming Settlement Smashers’ authorization and procedural framework for providing consulting services. It summarizes the company’s licensing, contractual authority, and oversight practices to ensure compliance with professional service delivery standards.

Evidence Summary
Authorization to Provide Consulting Services —
Settlement Smashers maintains current business registration and professional licensing authorizing the firm to provide consulting services in all relevant jurisdictions. Certificates of registration and renewal confirmations are on file with the State Business Regulatory Office.

Scope of Services —
The scope of services is defined under the Professional Services Charter and standardized Statement of Work (SOW) templates that specify engagement limits, client types, and service boundaries.

Contractual Authority —
All client engagements are governed by Master Service Agreements (MSAs) with designated signature authority defined in the authorization matrix. Officers with contract approval limits are reviewed annually by the compliance department.

Ethics and Compliance Oversight —
All consulting staff must complete annual ethics, confidentiality, and data-handling training prior to client engagement. Records are retained in the corporate HR Compliance System.

Record Retention and Oversight —
Project documentation, contracts, and client communications are stored in the Consulting Engagement Management System (CEMS) with restricted access and quarterly compliance review.

Supporting Documentation
1. Business Registration and Licensing Certificates — Official documents verifying authorization to operate.
2. Professional Services Charter — Defines scope and consulting boundaries.
3. Master Service Agreements — Samples with authorization and approval logs.
4. Training Records — Certificates confirming annual ethics and compliance completion.
5. CEMS Audit Reports — Records demonstrating access tracking and document integrity.

Observations
* All licenses and registrations remain current.
* Contractual authority matrix validated during annual compliance review.
* Ethics and confidentiality training completed companywide.
* Records demonstrate consistent internal oversight.
